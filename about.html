About me

GIS shaped by movement, places, and public service.

My path from South Africa to Minnesota sparked a lasting interest in transportation, cities, infrastructure, and the connections between people and place.

My background

I was born in Port Elizabeth—now Gqeberha—in South Africa’s Eastern Cape, and spent much of my childhood in Zinyaville near Rustenburg before moving to Minneapolis in 2014.

After arriving in Minnesota, I relied regularly on Metro Transit buses and the Blue Line. Those everyday journeys grew into an interest in transportation systems, accessibility, urban planning, and geographic connections.

I earned my Bachelor of Science in Geographic Information Science from the University of Minnesota in May 2026. My studies covered cartography, spatial analysis, geocomputing, remote sensing, network analysis, environmental analysis, and web mapping.
