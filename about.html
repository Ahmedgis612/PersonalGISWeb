About Me

GIS Graduate | Spatial Analyst | Geospatial Professional

I am a Geographic Information Science graduate from the University of Minnesota with experience in infrastructure mapping, spatial analysis, transportation research, data management, and web GIS.

My Background

My name is Ahmed Mohamed. I was born in Port Elizabeth, now known as Gqeberha, in the Eastern Cape of South Africa. I later lived in Zinyaville near Rustenburg, where I spent much of my childhood before moving to Minneapolis in 2014.

Living in both South Africa and Minnesota shaped my interest in communities, transportation, cities, infrastructure, and the ways people interact with the places around them.

After moving to Minneapolis, I attended Roosevelt High School and regularly relied on Metro Transit buses and the Blue Line. Those experiences helped develop my interest in transportation systems, accessibility, urban planning, and geographic connections.

Education

I earned my Bachelor of Science in Geographic Information Science from the University of Minnesota in May 2026.

During my studies, I developed experience in cartography, spatial analysis, geocomputing, remote sensing, network analysis, transportation geography, environmental analysis, urban climatology, and web-based mapping.

My academic work included projects involving transit performance, crime mapping, retail service areas, public health, environmental conditions, infrastructure, and geographic storytelling.

Professional GIS Experience

GIS Engineering Intern

Saint Paul Regional Water Services — Engineering Division, Maps and Records

In this role, I support infrastructure-related GIS work using ArcGIS Pro and ArcGIS Online. My responsibilities include updating spatial data, uploading completed fieldwork documents, reviewing geographic information, performing data quality control, and helping maintain accurate infrastructure records.

This experience has strengthened my understanding of enterprise GIS, utilities, asset management, engineering workflows, and the importance of reliable geographic data in public service.
